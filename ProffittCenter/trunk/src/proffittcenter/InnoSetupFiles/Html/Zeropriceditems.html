Zero priced items

Items that have a zero price recorded cause an Enter price in pence message to be displayed, when sold. This does not change the price of the product permanently; just the price for this sale. Such items can be used for goods which are counted for stock control, but which do not have a fixed price. Loose cut cheese might be an example. Price encoded bar codes are a better solution, where this is possible. Another example would be live (aquarium) fish which are charged an amount depending on their size. Barcodes are not practical, here.
