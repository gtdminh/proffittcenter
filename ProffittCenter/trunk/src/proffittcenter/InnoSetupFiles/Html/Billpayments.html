Bill payments

In the UK there is a service called PayPoint that many shops participate in, that allows money to be collected for various bill payment facilities. There are also competitors who offer a similar service, and no doubt the same service is offered in other countries. The money collected is not a part of the shop's turnover; the shop is collecting it on behalf of service as an agent and for this reason, the payments are referred to in ProffittCenter as Agency payments. They are identified by being given a tax code of 6 - Excluded. The term Excluded can be re-named, but the ID must remain unchanged. Any product with an Excluded tax rate will not be included in the shop's takings and a separate tally will be maintained in the Takings screen.

Commonly, a hotkey will be used, as the payments may be variable. We used the letter U for utility, so that to pay Â£10, one entered u1000.

When the money has been collected, the payment details are entered into the terminal provided by the service and the transaction completed. It is normally complicated to reverse a payment with the system, so it is important to collect the money before entering the details. For this reason, the amounts involved are shown after the sale is complete in the same space as the date is normally shown.

Bill payments cannot be paid by cheque or debit card unless authorised by an owner or manager. For individual bill payments, the *n and blank entry (increase by one) will not work.
