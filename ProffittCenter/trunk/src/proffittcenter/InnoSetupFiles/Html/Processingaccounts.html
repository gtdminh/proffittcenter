Processing accounts

For products that are charged, sell as normal and then scan the Charged barcode or type 1000034 followed by the enter key, or use the menu option Payment>Charge. If a customer has not been defined, the Customers screen  is displayed. Select the name of the desired customer with the mouse and then press the OK button. A new customer entry can be created clicking the New customer button.

Subsequently, accounts must be paid for if you are to remain in business. On an empty sales screen, scan the Received on account barcode, or type 1000035, or use the menu option Payment>Received on account. The Customers by name  screen will be displayed to select the correct customer. Alternatively, in this screen, a customer account card carrying a customer number as a barcode can be scanned, to obviate the need to manually select a customer. Then the outstanding balance will be shown and any amount may be tendered in cash, card or cheque, to reduce this. Any remaining balance will be carried forward. If zero is entered as a tender, the sales screen is cleared, allowing for a quick account balance check.

The barcodes for charged and received on account can be printed out using the  option File>Print barcodes.

Two keyboard shortcuts are provided for these functions: < (cash in, received on account) for Received on account and > (goods out, charged) for Charged. Just press the key wherever you might scan the barcode for the function.

In some situations you do not wish to extend credit to a customer but want some provision for the customer to pay in advance, and to allow purchases to be deducted from this amount. This can be done by the menu option Payment>Credit customer, by scanning the Credit customer barcode or typing 1000043 and then pressing Enter. The customer needs a credit limit of zero, if credit is not to be extended, once the money is spent. When the customer has a positive balance, the figure for balance will appear as a negative amount.
