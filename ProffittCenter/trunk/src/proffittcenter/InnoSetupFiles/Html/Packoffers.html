Pack offers

Pack offers are offers that apply to one or more products, where a discount is offered whenever a specified number of items are sold. This is best clarified by a few examples. Suppose we wish to sell four packs of Stella at a discount of 20p from the cost of four individual cans. This equates to 5p a can. However, if five cans are purchased, there is no discount given on the fifth can. If a discount were to be required on the fifth can, then a quantity discount is the required offer type. Another example would be the offer of buying any four bars from a range of six chocolate bars for a pound. This can be applied by giving an offer price of 25p to each of the six products. A range of products can be selected for the offer. Consider a cat food - there might be a range of six flavours all covered by the same offer.

To set up a pack offer, choose the menu selection Offers|Create and then choose Pack offer in the drop down box.

The required first product is scanned, or the alpha lookup function used. Pressing tab moves the focus to the quantity for the offer and then the limit. Leave the limit as zero if there is no limit. Limit values do not stop the sale of more items - they limit the number of items that can be bought at a discount. Additional items are sold at the full price. The normal price will be filled in when enter is pressed.. The offer price will be the price for the pack. In the example of the bars of chocolate above, this would be Â£1. Next, the start and end dates are selected by means of a drop down calendars. Offers only have effect between these dates. If more than one product is required for the offer, click the Add button (or type + in the product box) and then enter the additional product. It is not then be necessary to set a price for the offer. The offer will be automatically inactivated on the day after the end date.
